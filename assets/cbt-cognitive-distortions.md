# CBT Cognitive Distortions

## Source: David Burns - "Feeling Good" (1980)

### 1. All-or-Nothing Thinking
One mistake means complete failure. No middle ground.
**Text markers:** "I always fail", "I never get it right", "This completely ruined everything"

### 2. Overgeneralization
Single negative event = never-ending pattern.
**Text markers:** "This always happens to me", "I'll never be able to...", "Every time I try..."

### 3. Mental Filter
Pick out single negative detail, dwell exclusively.
**Text markers:** Focusing only on what went wrong, dismissing positives as "luck"

### 4. Disqualifying the Positive
Reject positive experiences, "don't count."
**Text markers:** "That doesn't count because...", "Anyone could have done that"

### 5. Jumping to Conclusions
**a) Mind Reading:** "He thinks I'm stupid"
**b) Fortune Telling:** "This is going to be a disaster"

### 6. Magnification/Minimization
Exaggerate negatives, shrink positives.
**Text markers:** "This is the worst thing ever", "My [achievement]? That's nothing"

### 7. Emotional Reasoning
Emotions = reality. "I feel guilty so I must have done something wrong."
**Text markers:** "I feel like..." treated as evidence

### 8. Should Statements
Rigid rules about how things should be.
**Text markers:** "I should have known", "I must always...", "A good trader would..."

### 9. Labeling
Instead of "I made a mistake" → "I'm a loser."
**Text markers:** "I'm such an idiot", "What a failure"

### 10. Personalization
See yourself as cause of negative events you didn't cause.
**Text markers:** "It's all my fault", "They failed because of me"

## Automatic Negative Thoughts (ANTs)

**Characteristics:**
1. Automatic - appear without effort
2. Rapid - flash through mind
3. Believable - feel obviously true
4. Often unnoticed - operate below awareness

**Common ANTs:**
- "I'm not good enough"
- "I'm a fraud and they'll find out"
- "This will end badly"
- "They're going to reject me"
- "I can't handle this"
