# Defense Mechanisms - Academic Sources

## George Vaillant - Harvard Grant Study

> "Defense mechanisms are unconscious psychological processes that protect us from anxiety and emotional pain. The crucial insight is that defenses exist on a continuum from pathological to mature."

## Four Levels of Defense Maturity

### Level 1 - Pathological
- Delusional projection
- Psychotic denial
- Distortion

### Level 2 - Immature
- **Acting out** - Direct expression of impulse without reflection
- **Passive aggression** - Indirect resistance
- **Fantasy** - Retreat into imagination
- **Projection** - Attributing own feelings to others

### Level 3 - Neurotic
- **Displacement** - Redirect emotion to safer target
- **Intellectualization** - Use thinking to avoid feeling
- **Repression** - Push unacceptable thoughts to unconscious
- **Reaction formation** - Act opposite of true feeling
- **Dissociation** - Disconnect from experience

### Level 4 - Mature
- **Sublimation** - Channel impulse into productive activity
- **Humor** - Find comic aspects in situation
- **Suppression** - Consciously postpone dealing with issue
- **Altruism** - Help others to manage own distress

## Key Mechanisms for Wendy to Recognize

**Intellectualization:**
> "The trader who can brilliantly analyze their losses while feeling nothing is intellectualizing. It looks like insight but it's actually a defense against insight. True insight is felt, not just thought."

**Acting Out:**
> "The person who slams their keyboard after a losing trade is acting out. The person who revenge trades is acting out. The function is to discharge tension immediately rather than tolerate the discomfort."

**Projection:**
> "Whenever the emotional charge exceeds the situation, projection is present. If someone describes a coworker with unusual intensity they're seeing their own disowned qualities."

**Rationalization:**
> "That's not 'strategic retreat'. That's you being scared. It's okay to be scared. But don't give cowardice a nice name."
