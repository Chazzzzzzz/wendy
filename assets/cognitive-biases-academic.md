# Cognitive Biases - Academic Sources

## Loss Aversion

**Source: Kahneman & Tversky, "Prospect Theory" (1979)**

> "The value function is steeper for losses than for gains. The aggravation that one experiences in losing a sum of money appears to be greater than the pleasure associated with gaining the same amount."

**The 2.5x Ratio:**
Losses hurt about twice as much as gains feel good.

**Text markers for Wendy:**
- "I can't sell at a loss"
- "It has to come back"
- "I'll wait until I'm at least even"
- "Unrealized losses aren't real losses"
- Moving stop-losses down
- Adding to losing positions to "average down"

---

## Confirmation Bias

**Source: Peter Wason (1960)**

> "Subjects showed a systematic bias toward seeking evidence that confirms rather than disconfirms their hypotheses."

**Text markers:**
- "All the indicators show..."
- "Everything points to..."
- "This one analyst agrees with me"
- Dismissing contradicting evidence as "noise"
- Only citing sources that support position
- "The market is wrong"

---

## Recency Bias

**Source: Tversky & Kahneman, "Availability Heuristic" (1973)**

> "There is a tendency to overweight recent experiences in estimating frequency or probability."

**Text markers:**
- "This time is different"
- "The last 3 trades all won so..."
- "I've been losing lately so the system doesn't work"
- Unable to reference longer time frames
- Panic after 2-3 losses in a row
- Overconfidence after 2-3 wins in a row

---

## Sunk Cost Fallacy

**Source: Arkes & Blumer (1985)**

> "The sunk cost effect is manifested in a greater tendency to continue an endeavor once an investment in money, effort, or time has been made."

**Text markers:**
- "I've already put X into this"
- "I've spent X months/years on this"
- "I can't quit now, I've come too far"
- "Selling now would mean the loss was real"
- "I need to make back what I lost"
- Adding to losers to "average down"
