# Attachment Theory - Academic Sources

## John Bowlby - Core Theory

**Internal Working Models:**
- Infant/caregiver relationship → forms template for all relationships
- "I am lovable" vs "I am unworthy"
- "Others are reliable" vs "Others will abandon me"

## Mary Ainsworth - Four Types

### Secure (~60%)
- Believe self is worthy of love
- Believe others are reliable
- Can be independent AND intimate
- Good emotional regulation

### Anxious-Preoccupied (~20%)
- Need constant reassurance
- Fear of abandonment
- Over-sensitive to rejection signals
- Can overwhelm others
- **Trading:** Constantly checking account, needs confirmation, can't leave screen

### Dismissive-Avoidant (~15%)
- Appear independent, don't need others
- Actually suppress intimacy needs
- Flee when relationships deepen
- **Trading:** Over-confident, won't take advice, lone wolf

### Fearful-Avoidant (~5%)
- Want intimacy but fear it
- Chaotic relationship patterns
- Often from childhood trauma
- **Trading:** In and out of markets, can't commit to system

## Peter Fonagy - Mentalization

> "Mentalization is the capacity to understand behavior in terms of underlying mental states. When mentalization fails, we treat internal states as physical realities. The trade that goes against us isn't just a loss; it feels like an attack."

## Application for Wendy

**Anxious trader signs:**
- Checks positions obsessively
- Needs external validation for decisions
- Can't hold positions through normal drawdowns
- "What do you think I should do?"

**Avoidant trader signs:**
- Won't admit mistakes
- "I don't need anyone's advice"
- Cuts off information sources when losing
- Can't ask for help

**The question:**
"How do you handle it when you're wrong? Do you reach out for support or isolate? Do you need others to tell you it's okay, or do you refuse to let anyone see you struggle?"
