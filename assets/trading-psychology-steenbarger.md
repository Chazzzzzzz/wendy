# Trading Psychology - Brett Steenbarger

## The Most Important Skill

> "The single most important psychological skill in trading is the capacity to be our own observer. It's the ability to watch ourselves even as we're engaged in the markets, noticing our thoughts, feelings, and impulses without being controlled by them."

## Why Traders Fail to Execute

> "Execution problems rarely stem from lack of knowledge. Traders know what they should do. The problem is psychological: under stress, we regress to earlier patterns of coping. The trader who researched carefully suddenly becomes the child who doesn't want to be wrong."

## Trading and Attachment Styles

> "Your attachment history shows up in your trading whether you know it or not. The anxiously attached trader can't leave positions alone. The avoidant trader refuses to acknowledge losses. The secure trader can hold through fluctuation and exit without making it mean something about their worth."

## Mark Douglas - Trading in the Zone

**On Accepting Risk:**
> "The best traders have learned to accept the risk of trading completely. They don't avoid, hedge, or rationalize it away. They've made peace with the fact that any trade can lose. This is not resignation. It's liberation."

**On the Trader's Edge:**
> "Your edge only manifests over a series of trades. You never know which trades will be winners. The amateur confuses having an edge with knowing what will happen. The professional understands that 'knowing your edge' means knowing your probabilities over a large sample."

## Key Trading Psychology Patterns

**Tilt:**
Definition: Emotional takeover after loss
Signs: Increased position size, revenge trading, abandoning stops

**Revenge Trading:**
Definition: Trying to "win back" losses
Root: Wounded ego > wounded account

**FOMO:**
Definition: Fear of missing out
Root: Exclusion fear, not-good-enough anxiety

**Analysis Paralysis:**
Definition: Can't act due to over-analysis
Root: Fear of decision, perfectionism

**Overconfidence After Wins:**
Definition: Feeling invincible after streak
Root: Confusing luck with skill
